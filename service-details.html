FAQ

Freequently Asked Questions

  • What are CCTV Cameras?

    CCTV (Closed Circuit Television) cameras are surveillance cameras used for monitoring and recording activities in specific areas. They're commonly employed for security purposes in homes, businesses, and public spaces.

  • How Do Surveillance Cameras Work?

    Surveillance cameras capture video footage of their surroundings and transmit it to a recording device or monitoring station. They use sensors to detect changes in light and motion, converting them into electrical signals that create the video image.

  • What Types of Surveillance Cameras are Available?

    Various types of surveillance cameras include analog cameras, IP cameras, PTZ (Pan-Tilt-Zoom) cameras, thermal imaging cameras, and smart HD CVI cameras. Each type offers unique features and benefits suited to different surveillance needs.

  • How Can I Maintain and Troubleshoot CCTV Cameras?

    Maintain CCTV cameras by regularly cleaning lenses, checking connections, and updating firmware. Troubleshooting steps may include checking power sources, adjusting camera settings, and inspecting cables for damage.

  • What Factors Should I Consider When Choosing CCTV Cameras?

    Factors to consider when choosing CCTV cameras include resolution (such as HD or 4K), lens type (fixed or varifocal), field of view, night vision capabilities, weather resistance, and connectivity options (analog or IP).

  • What Are the Benefits of Remote Monitoring and Mobile Access?

    Remote monitoring and mobile access allow users to view live camera feeds, receive alerts, and control cameras from anywhere with an internet connection. This enables real-time surveillance and quick response to security events.
